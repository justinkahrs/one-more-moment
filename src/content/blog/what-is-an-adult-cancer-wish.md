Many people understand wish granting for children. Fewer people realize that adults facing cancer may also need meaningful wish support.

An adult cancer wish is a meaningful experience or Moment created for an adult facing Stage IV, advanced, or metastatic cancer. It is a chance to make time for the people, places, conversations, and memories that matter most.

One More Moment helps adults in Michigan facing Stage IV, advanced, or metastatic cancer create meaningful Moments with the people they love. We use the word Moment because the heart of each wish experience is time: time to connect, rest, remember, laugh, say what needs to be said, or simply be together.

For a broader overview of adult cancer wishes in Michigan, visit our guide to [wish granting for adults with cancer in Michigan](/adult-cancer-wishes-michigan/).

## What an Adult Cancer Wish Can Look Like

An adult cancer wish can be simple or more involved. It does not have to follow one format, and it does not need to look impressive from the outside to be meaningful.

A Moment might include:

- Dinner with loved ones
- A visit to a favorite place
- A short Michigan getaway
- A family gathering
- A meaningful conversation
- A quiet day together
- Time outdoors or near the water
- A chance to reconnect with someone important

The meaning comes from the person, their relationships, and what matters most to them. For one person, a Stage IV cancer wish may be a peaceful day at home with family. For another, it may be a trip to the lakeshore, a meal at a favorite restaurant, or one more chance to gather the people they love in the same room.

## Adult Cancer Wishes Do Not Have to Be Large or Expensive

An adult cancer wish does not have to be a big trip, a public event, or an expensive experience.

Cancer can make ordinary things harder. A meal, a visit, a short drive, or a quiet afternoon may require planning, transportation, money, energy, and help from others. When those barriers are removed, the person and their loved ones can focus on being present.

That is why meaningful Moments are not measured by cost. A simple Moment can carry enormous value when it gives someone time with the people they love.

If you are wondering what kinds of wishes may be possible, read [What Kind of Wishes Can Adults With Cancer Request?](/blog/what-kind-of-wishes-can-adults-with-cancer-request/).

## Dignity Is Part of the Moment

Late-stage cancer support is not only about appointments, treatment plans, and practical needs. It is also about helping someone be seen as more than their diagnosis.

A person facing Stage IV, advanced, or metastatic cancer is still a spouse, partner, parent, grandparent, sibling, child, friend, neighbor, storyteller, music lover, sports fan, lake person, home cook, or the person everyone calls when they need honest advice.

A thoughtful Moment respects that full person. It centers their choices, comfort, energy, and relationships. It gives them room to feel known, valued, and included in a way that illness can sometimes make harder.

## Memories Can Carry Forward

A Moment may only last a few hours or a day, but loved ones may carry the memory forward for years.

Families often remember the small details: who sat where at dinner, the story that made everyone laugh, the way the lake looked that afternoon, the hand held during a quiet conversation, or the relief of having time together without rushing.

The goal is not to create pressure for a perfect day. Real Moments can be quiet, imperfect, funny, emotional, and deeply meaningful all at once. What lasts is the connection.

## Privacy Matters

Some Moments are shared publicly only with permission. Many remain private.

One More Moment respects the privacy of every applicant, family, and referral partner. A family does not need to share photos, names, medical details, or a public story for a Moment to matter. Privacy can help people relax and experience the day on their own terms.

You can learn more about how submitted information is handled in the [Privacy Policy](/privacy-policy/).

## Who Can Request or Help Request a Moment

A Moment request may come directly from the person facing cancer, but they do not have to do it alone.

A request or referral may come from:

- The person facing cancer
- A spouse or partner
- A family member
- A friend
- A caregiver
- A hospice worker
- An oncology team member
- A social worker
- A church or community contact

If you know someone in Michigan facing Stage IV, advanced, or metastatic cancer, you can start with the [Request a Moment](/moments/#request-a-moment) form. You can also review [Who Qualifies for a One More Moment Wish?](/blog/who-qualifies-for-a-one-more-moment-wish/) before submitting.

## How Donors and Supporters Help

Wish granting for adults with cancer takes care, coordination, and resources. Donors and supporters help create time, remove barriers, and make meaningful experiences possible.

Support may help with travel, lodging, meals, accessibility needs, gathering loved ones, or other practical pieces connected to a Moment. It can also help a family say yes to an experience that would otherwise feel out of reach.

Because corporate donors and generous families/supporters help cover overhead, **100% of public donations go directly toward wish granting.**

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is an adult cancer wish?

An adult cancer wish is a meaningful experience or Moment created for an adult facing Stage IV, advanced, or metastatic cancer. It may be simple or more involved, but it should reflect the person, their relationships, and what matters most to them.

### Who qualifies for an adult cancer wish?

One More Moment supports adults in Michigan who are facing Stage IV, advanced, or metastatic cancer and who need help creating a meaningful Moment with loved ones. Every request is reviewed with care, and approval, funding, timing, and availability cannot be guaranteed.

### Does an adult cancer wish have to be a big trip?

No. A Moment does not have to be large or expensive. Dinner with loved ones, a quiet day together, a visit to a favorite place, or time near the water can be deeply meaningful.

### Can someone else request a Moment for an adult with cancer?

Yes. A spouse or partner, family member, friend, caregiver, hospice worker, oncology team member, social worker, church contact, or community contact may help request or refer someone. The person facing cancer should remain central to the Moment being requested.

### Are adult cancer wishes shared publicly?

Only with permission. Some Moments are shared publicly, but many remain private. A private Moment is still meaningful and fully valued.

### How can someone request a Moment in Michigan?

Start with the [Request a Moment](/moments/#request-a-moment) form. You can also review [who qualifies](/blog/who-qualifies-for-a-one-more-moment-wish/) or contact One More Moment at [contactus@onemoremoment.org](mailto:contactus@onemoremoment.org) with questions.

## About One More Moment

**One More Moment** grants meaningful wish experiences for adults in Michigan who are facing Stage IV, advanced, or metastatic cancer and who lack the resources to create that Moment on their own.
