From the outside, a Moment may look like a dinner, a short trip, a visit, or a favorite activity.

To the person facing cancer and the people who love them, it can mean much more.

The experience itself matters, but it is often only the setting for what the family truly needs: time together, a meaningful conversation, relief from planning, the comfort of familiar people, or the chance to feel present without making cancer the center of the day.

One More Moment helps adults in Michigan facing Stage IV, advanced, or metastatic cancer create meaningful Moments with the people they love. These adult cancer wishes are not about pretending that illness, pain, stress, or grief disappears. They are about making room for connection, dignity, memory, and time together in the middle of a difficult season.

For a broader overview of adult cancer wishes in Michigan, visit our guide to [wish granting for adults with cancer in Michigan](/adult-cancer-wishes-michigan/).

## Adult Cancer Wishes Are About Connection

The activity may bring people together, but the connection is often what gives the Moment its meaning.

A dinner creates a place to sit around the same table. A visit gives people space to talk or simply enjoy being near one another. A trip can change the scenery and help a family step outside its usual routine. A favorite activity can bring back laughter, stories, and a familiar part of the person's life.

Connection can look like:

- Being with the people who matter most
- Sharing a meal without rushing
- Having a conversation that has been hard to make time for
- Laughing about a family story
- Sitting quietly together
- Feeling present instead of focused on the next task

There is no single correct way for a family to connect. Some people want a lively gathering. Others want a quiet day with one or two loved ones. The best Moment is the one that reflects the person and the relationships that matter to them.

## Adult Cancer Wishes Are About Dignity

Cancer can take up a great deal of space. Appointments, tests, treatments, schedules, symptoms, paperwork, and practical decisions can begin to shape how a person spends their time and how others speak to them.

But a person is always more than a diagnosis.

They are still a spouse, partner, parent, grandparent, sibling, friend, neighbor, music lover, lake person, storyteller, traveler, home cook, sports fan, or keeper of the family's favorite traditions.

A thoughtful Stage IV cancer wish can honor that fuller person. It can reflect what they enjoy, where they feel comfortable, and who they want beside them. It can give them choices in a season when many choices may feel limited.

Dignity also means respecting energy, accessibility, privacy, and changing needs. A Moment should never require someone to perform happiness or make the experience look perfect. It should give the person room to be themselves.

## Adult Cancer Wishes Are About Time

Time is one of the most important parts of a Moment.

It may be time away from logistics, appointments, coordination, and stress. It may be time with family members who live far apart. It may be a few hours to enjoy a meal, return to a favorite place, or take part in something meaningful.

Wish granting for adults with cancer can help protect that time by handling details that would otherwise fall on the person or family. When someone else helps arrange transportation, coordinate a plan, or cover an expense, loved ones can spend less energy managing the day and more energy being together.

A Moment does not pause cancer or promise a certain emotional outcome. It can, however, create a little more room for the person and family to decide how they want to spend that time.

## Adult Cancer Wishes Are About Memory

A Moment may last an afternoon, an evening, or a day. The memory can stay with loved ones much longer.

Families may remember the place, but they often carry smaller details too: who told the funniest story, what everyone ordered, the song playing in the car, the view across the water, or the feeling of having the right people together.

Those memories do not erase hard experiences. They become part of the family's larger story—one that includes love, personality, humor, and connection alongside everything cancer has brought.

There is no need to pressure a family into creating a perfect memory. Real Moments can be quiet, imperfect, emotional, funny, or different from what everyone expected. What matters is that the experience belongs to the person and the people they love.

## Removing Barriers Creates Room for the Moment

Even a simple idea can be difficult to arrange when a family is managing late-stage cancer.

Common barriers may include:

- Cost
- Transportation
- Coordination between family members
- Planning stress
- Accessibility or comfort needs
- Finding trusted local connections

Late-stage cancer support can include removing some of those practical burdens. One More Moment works to understand what is getting in the way and what may help make the requested experience possible.

For one family, support might mean covering a meal. For another, it could mean helping with travel, lodging, tickets, or local coordination. The details are different because each person and each Moment is different.

## Simple Moments Can Carry Deep Meaning

A Moment does not have to be large or expensive to matter.

A dinner can bring loved ones together around a familiar table. A visit can reconnect people separated by distance. A trip to a favorite Michigan place can bring back years of memories. A quiet day can let someone rest, talk, laugh, or simply enjoy having family nearby.

Simple Moments may include:

- A favorite meal with loved ones
- A visit from someone important
- Time at a lake, park, restaurant, or family place
- A short getaway close to home
- A favorite activity
- A peaceful day with family or friends

The size of the plan does not determine the depth of the experience. Learn more in [What Kind of Wishes Can Adults With Cancer Request?](/blog/what-kind-of-wishes-can-adults-with-cancer-request/).

## Private Moments Matter Too

Not every Moment is shared publicly.

Some people and families are comfortable sharing their names, photographs, or stories. Others want the experience to remain within their own circle. Both choices are respected.

Moments are shared publicly only with permission. A private Moment is not less meaningful because fewer people see it. In some cases, privacy is part of what allows the person and family to feel comfortable and fully present.

Families should never feel pressure to turn a personal experience into public content. More information about how submitted information is handled is available in the [Privacy Policy](/privacy-policy/).

## Donors Support More Than an Activity

When someone supports a Moment, they are not only funding a dinner, visit, trip, or event.

They are helping create time. They are helping remove a barrier. They are helping a person feel seen as more than a diagnosis. They are helping loved ones be together in a way that may not have been possible without support.

The activity is visible. The connection, dignity, and memory within it are the deeper reasons the support matters.

Because corporate donors and generous families/supporters help cover overhead, **100% of public donations go directly toward wish granting.**

## How to Request a Moment

If you know an adult in Michigan facing Stage IV, advanced, or metastatic cancer, begin with the [Request a Moment](/moments/#request-a-moment) form.

The request does not need to be perfectly written or fully planned. Share enough to help One More Moment understand the person, what matters to them, and the Moment being considered. Every request is reviewed with care, though submitting a request does not guarantee approval, funding, timing, or availability.

You can review [Who Qualifies for a One More Moment Wish?](/blog/who-qualifies-for-a-one-more-moment-wish/) and [How to Request a Wish for an Adult With Stage 4 Cancer in Michigan](/blog/how-to-request-a-wish-for-an-adult-with-stage-4-cancer-in-michigan/) before submitting. Questions can be sent to [contactus@onemoremoment.org](mailto:contactus@onemoremoment.org).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Why are adult cancer wishes about more than the experience?

The dinner, trip, visit, or activity creates a setting for something deeper. A Moment can make room for connection, dignity, time together, and memories that loved ones may carry forward.

### How can a Moment help a family?

A Moment can remove practical barriers and give a family time to be together with less planning pressure. It does not remove illness or grief, but it can create space for a meaningful shared experience.

### Does a Moment have to be large or expensive?

No. A favorite dinner, a visit, time at a meaningful place, or a quiet day with loved ones can carry deep meaning. The right Moment is personal, not necessarily elaborate.

### Are private Moments still meaningful?

Yes. Some Moments are shared publicly only with permission, while others remain private. The number of people who see a Moment does not determine its value.

### How can someone support adult cancer wishes in Michigan?

One More Moment was inspired by Jason's personal experience with his mother's cancer journey and the realization that, near the end of life, even a simple moment together can mean everything. Through One More Moment, Jason and the organization work to provide dignity, connection, and meaningful experiences for adults and families navigating late-stage cancer.

## About One More Moment

**One More Moment** grants meaningful wish experiences for adults in Michigan who are facing Stage IV, advanced, or metastatic cancer and who lack the resources to create that Moment on their own.

Every request is reviewed with care. Eligibility may include Michigan residency, a qualifying cancer diagnosis, financial or logistical need, and confirmation from an oncology provider.
